FAQ

How does it work?

Pizza starts with a dough base, typically made from flour, water, yeast, and salt. Toppings like tomato sauce, cheese, and various ingredients are added. The pizza is then baked in an oven, creating a delicious and customizable dish.

How long does it take for delivery?

Pizza delivery times vary but usually take around 30 to 45 minutes. Factors like distance, traffic, and order volume can impact delivery speed. Check with the specific pizzeria for accurate estimates.

Can I order for huge parties?

Yes, many pizzerias offer catering services for large parties and events. Contact the restaurant in advance to discuss quantities, customization, and delivery options. Ensure a seamless experience for your big gathering with a tailored pizza order.

How much protein it contains?

The protein content in pizza varies based on toppings and crust, but a standard slice typically contains 12-15 grams. Protein sources include cheese, meat, and, in some cases, vegetable toppings. Check specific nutritional information from the pizzeria for precise details on protein content.

Is it cooked with oil?

Yes, pizza dough often contains olive oil or another type of oil for flavor and texture. Some pizzerias also brush the crust with oil before baking to enhance crispiness. Oil is a common ingredient in both the dough and preparation process of making pizza.
